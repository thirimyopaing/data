Batman: Why Three Jokers Is a PERFECT Joker War Sequel

Joker War and Three Jokers serve as complementary companion pieces that has Batman's greatest enemy challenge him in very different ways.

Batman: Why Three Jokers Is a PERFECT Joker War Sequel
Two very different stories that revolved around Batman's eternal conflict with the Joker have recently come to a close in James Tynion IV and Jorge Jimenez's "Joker War" and Geoff Johns and Jason Fabok's Batman: Three Jokers. The two tales revolved around the Clown Prince of Crime coming at the Dark Knight and Bat-Family in a more personal way than ever, as the supervillain was well aware of the team's various secret identities. Despite their differences, the two stories work perfectly as companion pieces exploring Batman and the Joker's never-ending battle, especially with Johns confirming he regards Three Jokers as canon to the main DC Universe."
In regards to canon, when Three Jokers and "Joker War" take place in relation to one another is one of those continuity questions perhaps better left unexplored. Alfred Pennyworth appears alive and well throughout the course of Three Jokers while his death looms large over the Bat-Family during the events of "Joker War." Jim Gordon doesn't know his daughter's identity as Batgirl while Barbara tacitly reveals her double life to him in a moment of anger at the end of Three Jokers. Finally, Jason Todd drops his Red Hood persona at the end of Three Jokers to impress Barbara but is seen dropping the alter ego under much different circumstances in immediate aftermath of "Joker War."

Jasper's Warp: How Alan Moore Helped Shape the X-Men's World

Legendary writer Alan Moore left an indelible mark on the world of the X-Men with the story "Jasper's Warp" which reshaped the world of the mutants.

Jasper's Warp: How Alan Moore Helped Shape the X-Men's World
As the X-Men prepare for battle in X Of Swords, the newly reconstituted Otherworld is on display. The arena for the great tournament finds many of its roots in a run of Captain Britain stories, helmed by one of the most renowned comic book writers living today, Alan Moore.Captain Britain who derives his powers from the Amulet of Right, bequeathed to him by supernatural beings of English lore, Merlyn and his daughter Roma, has often been depicted as part of a larger multiversal Captain Britain Corps that gets introduced for the first time here.
When Brian Braddock, the Captain Britain of Earth-616 faces The Fury head on, he loses his life as well. Merlin and Roma manage to reconstruct and resurrect Brian, sending him back to his original Earth in the process. From here he reunites with his sister, Elizabeth Braddock, now sporting purple hair. Not only would Betsy Braddock become Psylocke of X-Men fame, but she would later take up the Amulet of Right herself, serving as Captain Britain and one of Krakoa’s swordbearers in X Of Swords. It’s Moore who first explores her psychic and precognitive powers as a member of STRIKE, the corrupted British equivalent of SHIELD.

Captain America Defends New York From a Symbiote Invasion in Marvel's King in Black

Marvel Comics has revealed the cover and official synopsis for February's King in Black: Captain America #1.

Captain America Defends New York From a Symbiote Invasion in Marvel's King in Black
With Marvel Comics' King in Black event officially set to kick off next month, the House of Ideas has officially announced a new tie-in one-shot starring the Star-Spangled Man with a Plan, Captain American.Slated for release this coming February, King in Black: Captain America #1 comes from writer Danny Lore and artist Mirko Colak. The one-shot sees Steve Rogers/Captain America take to the streets of Manhattan in an effort to push back Knull's forces. Cap is joined in his battle against the God of the Symbiotes' army by longtime allies Bucky Barnes/Winter Soldier and Sam Wilson/Falcon..
"Steve, Bucky and Sam were some of the characters that got me back into comics as an adult, as the 616 universe grappled with Civil War," Lore said in an interview Adventures in Poor Taste. "Getting to write these very excellent dudes up against seemingly impossible odds is a dream come true. Right now, more than ever, it's really important for me to tell stories specifically about what happens when it seems like there's no way out, and I hope the readers find some comfort (and some really awesome horror!) in this one!"
